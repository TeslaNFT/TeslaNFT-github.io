Spell Checker is an Incident and Unit Narrative feature that automatically proofreads the Narrative and checks for spelling errors, provides suggestions for misspelled words, then lets you change delete or leave the misspelled word unchanged.

CD-15 Operations Report Guidelines


Battalion Operations Report

The battalion chief must complete a CD-15 whenever:

  • The Battalion Chief performs duty, or is involved in an accident or other unusual circumstances at an alarm, to which the battalion is not the first due chief.

  • The battalion chief responds to a second or higher alarm.


    The Battalion Chief completing a CD-15 must include:

  • The name of the chief officer reported to, and the name of the chief officer dismissed by.

  • A summary of the duty performed by units under direction of the battalion.

  • If control valves of an automatic sprinkler system were closed under his direction, the location of the valves, the name and unit of the members who closed them, and the approximate time of closing.


    Company Operations Report

    The company officer must complete a CD-15 for:

  • Any alarms which resulted in the company becoming involved in an accident or other unusual occurrence. (Injuries, fatal fires, lost Department or private property, etc.).

  • Second or greater alarms regardless of whether duty was performed or not.


    The company officer shall include the following that apply to the company's operations:

  • State particulars of accidents to apparatus and of injuries or deaths of members or other persons.

  • List names of members who closed valves of automatic sprinkler systems, and give location of valves and the approximate time closed.

  • For each ladder used, state its size and the exact nature and location of the operation performed.

  • State search procedures taken: State whether primary or secondary, location where performed and results.


    For each hose line stretched from the company's own unit, give the following information:

  • Where and how the line was taken to the point of operation.

  • If it was stretched through buildings other than the fire structure, give their addresses.

  • Which unit operated the line, which unit the hose came from, and whether the line was turned over to another unit or taken up.

  • Hose size and type, number of lengths, size and type of nozzle.

  • Location of stream and length of time operated.


    If the company operated a line from another unit, or relieved on a line, report:

  • Unit relieved.

  • The point of operation.

  • Length of time of operation.

  • To which company line was turned over, or whether it was taken up.

    SECURE AN INCIDENT WITH A PASSWORD

    In order to provide a means to secure sensitive information on fire reports and provide a higher level of privacy for these legal documents, a password may be assigned to selected incident reports. This will permit only the Battalion Chief “Responsible for the Incident” to access the report. Company officers will still be able to access and complete the CD-15 assigned to their company for that incident.


    The Chief of Operations and the Chief of Safety have defined the qualifying parameters for secured incident reports:

  • Fatality to a member.

  • Life threatening injury to a member.

  • A large number of civilian fatalities.

  • High Profile incidents.

  • Other incidents deemed necessary by the department.


When an incident falls within these parameters, the incident will be secured with a password assigned by either FDOC or the System Administrator once notified by Operations and/or Safety.


The decision to secure an Incident will be made by the Chief of Department, along with Chief of Operations or the Chief of Safety.


Any request from Chiefs in the field to have an Incident secured must be made through the Chief of Operations in writing.


When the Battalion Chief responsible for the incident attempts to access the report, the system will return the Warning: Secure Incident web page. The Battalion Chief will need to contact FDOC or the System Administrator for the password assigned to the incident.



47

NYFIRS User Manual Fire Department City of New York


The Battalion Chief responsible for the report will be issued the password, and this BC will be responsible for the security of the password.


Once the password is issued, only the Battalion Chief will be able to access the secure incident.


The Battalion Chief with the password will now have access to the secure incident as well as the CD-15’s submitted by the companies.


Once the Battalion Chief closes the secure incident report, the NYFIRS system will automatically send an e-mail to the Borough Commander informing them that the incident must be reviewed. The Borough Commander will need to contact FDOC for the password to review the incident.


Once the Borough Commander approves the completed Incident report they will be required to sign off on it. The report will now bear the Borough Commander's name, listing him as having reviewed the report. The name of the Battalion Chief who completed the report will be listed at the beginning of the narrative.


The “Public Incident Report” will be marked as “Under Review” until such time as the Borough Commander reviews the report and signs off on it.


If the Borough Commander does not agree or would like the Battalion Chief to add more information to the narrative he then will re-open the report. Once this is done the application will automatically send an e-mail back to the Battalion Chief responsible for the report. This will allow the Battalion Chief to make any necessary changes.


Changes in a Secure Incident – Company Level

On a company level, the changes for secure incidents are as follows:

When a CD-15 is opened and the member enters his tax registry number on the CD-15 assigned to his company, that company officer will be the only person to have access to that CD-15.

No other Unit will be able to view another company's CD-15 once an officer logs in and begins to complete it.

SYSTEM CONTROLS AND NAVIGATION


Color Key


NYFIRS screens are designed to be easy to use and to minimize learning time.

Background field colors denote common data types as specified by NYFIRS rules and guide the user through the reporting process.


Red

Red fields on the NYFIRS screens are required. Data must be entered in red fields in order to complete any given incident report in conformance with NYFIRS rules. These required fields vary depending on the incident type. As red fields are correctly completed the background color turns to white. Red lettering on any of the tabs indicates that one or more required fields have not been completed on that tab.

Dark Blue

Dark blue fields are read-only, meaning that you can't make any changes to the information in these fields.

Pale Blue

A pale blue field is the field that is currently "active," meaning that the cursor has been placed within this field and you can now type information or make a selection for this field.

White

White indicates active fields in which entries may be made as well as fields containing valid and NYFIRS-compliant data.

Yellow

Yellow indicates entries that are either invalid or not NYFIRS-compliant. When the errant entry is corrected the yellow background turns white.

Light Gray

Light gray fields are inactive fields in which data may not be entered. These fields vary

              depending on the incident being entered per NYFIRS rules.

Drop-down Lists

A Drop-down list is a data field with an attached list of acceptable values for selection. You may either type in an accepted entry option or select a value by clicking on the arrow.



For example, clicking on the arrow in the Type drop-down list in the Basic/Response tab opens a complete list of valid incident types. To select an option from the list, highlight it by placing the mouse cursor over it and then select it with a left mouse click.


If the list is too long to be displayed on the screen in its entirety, use the scroll bar control to scroll through the list. Click and hold the left mouse key on the scroll control and move it up and down to scroll through the list. When the left mouse button is released, the scrolled list will hold its position. Options, highlighted with a dark blue background, can be selected by striking the Enter key or with a single left mouse click. Drop-down list options can also be scrolled by the up and down cursor keys on the keyboard, which are marked by arrows.

Date/Time Controls

Date/Time controls found on the Basic/Response and Resources/Unit tabs are populated by CAD and are read only (cannot be changed).



Date/Time controls found on the Resources/Narrative, Fire Service/Involved and Civilian Casualty/Involved tabs will accept user input as will Times fields on the Basic/Summary tab.


Two characters are always used to indicate months and days and four characters are always used to indicate the year.

You can modify date characters directly within the date field two ways:

Click the date field and highlight the target numbers in pale yellow with a held down left mouse click. Enter the desired date to replace the existing data. Separators (/) are entered automatically. Use the backspace key to correct the data. You can also highlight the entire date field with your cursor. It will also be highlighted in pale yellow. Then enter the date. You may also use the scroll buttons to sequentially increase or decrease the date number to arrive at the desired date.



To change the month, highlight the number with your cursor and manually type in the desired month number. You may also use the scroll button to arrive at the desired month.



To change the year, highlight the number with your cursor and manually type in the desired year. You may also use the scroll button to arrive at the desired year.

Spin Controls

Spin controls or "spinners" are text boxes with up and down arrows that allow you to rapidly scroll through numeric values sequentially in ascending and descending order. Spinners are used by clicking and holding the left mouse button on the up-arrow (to scroll up) or down-arrow (to scroll down) until the number you want is displayed.



The following example shows three spin controls found on the Fire/ Property & Ignition tab; #Apts, Buildings and Acres. The values of each field are entered with a left click on the field to position the cursor and then a held left click on the up or down arrow to advance to the desired number.



If you prefer, you can type values in data fields with spin controls as follows: Place the cursor in the field with a left mouse click and then key in the numbers.

To change an existing value, highlight the value in yellow with the mouse cursor and then key in the desired numbers.


Spin controls are also built into a number of numeric data fields without the spinner button. These fields are controlled by the use of the arrows on your keyboard when the cursor is located in the data field.
